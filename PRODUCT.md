# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Solo freelancers and small business owners — non-technical or semi-technical operators drowning in repetitive, low-value work. They know AI exists but haven't found a structured, human way in. They come to this page skeptical of hype, tired of vague promises, and looking for something that feels built by a real person for real problems. The workshop is their first encounter with Till and with the idea that they could actually deploy their own agent.

## Product Purpose

A live 3-session workshop teaching solo entrepreneurs and small business owners to build and deploy their first personal AI agent — handling the tasks they hate. The landing page is the primary conversion surface for the first cohort (6 seats, July 2026). It is also the start of a growing product ecosystem (future cohorts, eventual course platform, portfolio of Till's AI automation work). Success means someone who lands on this page trusting enough to apply.

## Brand Personality

Warm craft, quiet confidence, earned expertise. Three words: **atmospheric, grounded, luminous**.

Till is not a hype merchant — he's a craftsman who's building the same thing he's teaching. The brand voice is direct and personal, not polished-corporate. The visual language earns its iridescence (the "cool glass" accent) by grounding it in warmth and human texture — not by leaning into the generic AI-purple.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / Vercel-style**: dark mode, hero metrics, purple gradient hero — the default AI startup aesthetic. The iridescent palette must never read as "another AI tool landing."
- **Corporate e-learning**: sterile grids, stock photography, trust badges, Coursera-sanitized neutrality.
- **Hustle-culture coaching**: countdown timers, aggressive CTAs, "10x your revenue," bold reds. No urgency theater.
- **Minimalist agency portfolio**: precious ultra-thin type on all-white — says nothing, feels cold. The warmth and craft must be present.

## Design Principles

1. **Earned iridescence.** The iris-violet/blue/cyan palette is the soul of the brand — but it earns its place by contrast with warmth and texture. Glass effects, orbs, and gradients only where they mean something. Never decorative by default.
2. **Human scale.** Headlines at human size, not shouting. Type that reads like it was written by a person, not generated. Copy that acknowledges the reader's skepticism.
3. **Show the craftsman.** Till made this — that fact should be visible in the texture, the attention to detail, the specificity of the content. Portfolio-quality execution, not template execution.
4. **Light touch, clear path.** The page is atmospheric but never confusing. The single action (apply) is always reachable. Information density rises as you scroll — don't dump it all upfront.
5. **Motion as atmosphere, not decoration.** Orbs float, sections breathe, reveals feel inevitable. Nothing performs — everything serves the feeling of calm, intentional craft.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 against canvas-1 (#FFFFFF).
- `prefers-reduced-motion`: all ambient float, parallax, and scroll animations freeze or crossfade. No content hidden behind animation triggers.
- Color not used as the only signal. Iridescent accents are decorative — never the only indicator of meaning.
- German-language audience (KI = Künstliche Intelligenz); type sizing should accommodate slightly longer German words.
